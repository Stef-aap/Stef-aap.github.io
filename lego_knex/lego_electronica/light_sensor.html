Just some notes about light sensing.

The Lego light sensor has been diassembled by the Japanese Sasuraibito, translation can be done by http://babel.altavista.com/translate.dyn (works great even on links).

Michael Gasperi also translated and interpreted the Japanese webpage.
A small correction to his interpretation: transitors 15 and 17 are just to compensate that the opamp LM358 not really can deal with input voltages down to 0 Volt (despite what the specifications say).

Another (short) disassembly report is written by Frank Angeli. He reports a fantastic improvent by removing the LED, this has been reported in newsgroups before.

Simen Svale Skogsrud used the main IR port to build a Lego Proximity Detector.

General information about photo-transistors can be found at IL300 application note (Infineon, formal Siemens),
also HP has some nice sites.

Ideas:

  • just pulse the LED during measurement, you can increase the power may be by a factor 10
  • do some code detection, so you can determine by software if the LED is on/off
  • use a sensor with a day-light filter
