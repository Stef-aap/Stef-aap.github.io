Lego Switch Multiplexer
Stef Mientki
april 2001

 
The best way to multiplex a number of switches is to create a current-source followed by a so called R-2R ladder network. With such a design you can even multiplex up to 8 (maybe 9) switches.
There will be a little problem caused by the internal 10 kOhm resistor of the RCX, but you compensate for that.

An even better idea is time-multiplexing, as done by Michael Gasperi and combining it with a current source.

On the right is a rough scetch of our Universal Control Box , which is a pratical compromise between the different principles.
Features

  • 3 potmeters
  • 10 switches
  • 3 external analog inputs
  • 3 status LEDs
  • test terminals
The design of Michael Gasperi.

You can extend this by a fourth resistor, to multiplex 4 switches. In that case practical resistor values are 10 kOhm, 33 kOhm, 68 kOhm, 150 kOhm.
