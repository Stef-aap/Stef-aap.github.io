Patient signals to be measured with FysioFlex-5


For all signals below yields:

- a good CMRR is necessary (realizing the the patient is connected through relative high (and different) impedance electrodes and the patient body is a perfect antenna for all kind of signals)

- medical safety, IEC60: leakage currents in single fault condition, 4kV double isolation.


The most common measured physiological signals,

measured with electrical electrodes are:


1.  ECG = Electro Cardiogram

amplitude 1 mV, bandwidth DC-200 Hz, resolution 1%


2.  EEG = Electro Encephalogram

amplitude 100 uV, bandwidth 1-200 Hz, resolution 1%


3.  EMG = Electro Myogram

amplitude 1mV, bandwidth 10-1000 Hz, resolution 1%


4.  EOG = Electro Ocularogram

amplitude 1V, bandwidth DC-10Hz, resolution 1%


There are a lot more signals that can be measured with electrodes,

but technical speaking they are a subset of the above signals,

or require special pre processing.


5.  Skin Conductance

Place 0.5 Volt across 2 patient electrodes and measure the conductance.

Most of the time we're only interested in the fast component:

amplitude 5 mV, bandwidth 1-20 Hz, resolution 1%

Sometimes also interested in slow component:

amplitude 500 mV, bandwidth DC-2 Hz, resolution 1%

So this either needs a 20 bit AD converter or a split channel design with different amplifications.


6.  Mercury Plethysmograph

A silicon tube filled with mercury, has a resistance of a few Ohms, with this you measure volume changes of about 1% by measuring the relative change. This requires special a high accurate measurement device or a normal, say 1% bridge amplifier with some calculations in the software.


7.  Normal grounded signal, e.g. from a signal generator

Amplitude 1V, bandwidth DC-1kHz, resolution 0.1%


8.  (Full) Bridges,

Exitation max 3V, Impedance 300 ... 1000 Ohm, generated signal 1 mV,

bandwidth DC-100Hz, resolution 1%


(9.  Breathing)

By measuring the thoracic impedance, with the same electrodes used to measure the ECG, injecting a 50 kHz 1uA signal.
