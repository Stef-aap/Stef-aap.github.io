IR sending

 

 

 

 

The most basic circuit.

You can always use 2 diodes in series, because voltage drop of IR is just 2 Volt.

Although this cicuit is the most simple and uses the minimum amount of pins, the software requires a lot of space and processor time.

The most elegant circuit.

Minimum of software requirements, just set the special PWM output to the desired modulation frequency and just send the characters to the USART.

The limitation of this circuit lies in the fact that only valid 8 or 9 bit RS232 compatible bytes can be sent (1 start bit, 1 or 2 stop bits).

The second best circuit.

The advantage over the previous circuit is that it's not bound to a 8 or 9 bit RS232 protocol.

In software you create any pulscode you like, while (the time consuming) high frequency modulation is done by the PWM output.

A circuit not in the "spirit" of  PICs, but it can save a pin and you get lost of the (time consuming) high frequency modulation.

  

  

 

IR Receiving

 

 

This is the standard circuit.

Note that the decoupling of the power supply by R1, C1 is very essential !!

R2 is not strictly necessary.

The optical receiver (TSOP ... or equivalent) decodes the high frequency modulation with the help of a lot of good tricks.  Because the data is not inverted, it can not directly be coupled into a PIC usart port.

The most elegant circuit.

The data is inverted, so there are a minimum of software requirements,  just read the characters from the USART.

The limitation of this circuit lies in the fact that only valid 8 or 9 bit RS232 compatible bytes can be received (1 start bit, 1 or 2 stop bits).
